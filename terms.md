## 2. What Misago is — and is not

Misago is an **informational tool** that helps owners of supported vehicles understand and plan repair and maintenance work. It answers questions in plain language and assembles step-by-step guidance grounded in the vehicle's factory service manual, citing the source where it can, and clearly labeling guidance that comes from general knowledge or web sources rather than the manual.

Misago's guidance is produced in part by **artificial intelligence**, and AI output **may be inaccurate, incomplete, or not appropriate for your specific vehicle's configuration or condition.** A source citation indicates where guidance was drawn from — it is **not** a warranty that the guidance is correct or complete. You should evaluate guidance for your circumstances and verify it before relying on it (see Section 3).

Misago is **a co-pilot, not a substitute** for the official factory service manual, for a qualified professional mechanic, or for your own judgment. It does not perform repairs, inspect your vehicle, or guarantee any outcome.

## 3. Safety, assumption of risk, and how Misago handles risk **(READ THIS SECTION)**

**Automotive repair is inherently dangerous.** Working on vehicles can cause serious injury, death, or property damage — from vehicles falling, fuel fires, stored energy, electrical hazards, or incorrect reassembly of safety-related systems. **You assume all risk** arising from your use of Misago and from any work you choose to perform.

You are solely responsible for:
- Assessing your own skill level, tools, and workspace before beginning any task, and stopping if a task is beyond your ability;
- **Verifying every step, specification, and torque value against your actual factory service manual** before you act on it — especially anything safety-related;
- Following all applicable safety practices (secure lifting and support, eye protection, fire safety, disconnecting power where appropriate); and
- The consequences of any repair you undertake.

**How Misago classifies work.** Misago reviews each task and assigns a risk level. For tasks it considers within skilled-DIY scope — including higher-risk jobs — it provides guidance together with safety call-outs and, where applicable, a required-equipment note; **you still proceed at your own risk and must verify against your manual.** For tasks it classifies as **restricted** (for example, airbag/SRS or high-voltage systems), Misago will **not** provide a procedure and will direct you to a qualified professional; any accompanying information is for understanding only.

**"Verify in your manual" notes.** Where Misago could not ground a specific detail in the factory manual, it flags that gap. You must look up those details in your manual or with a professional before relying on them.

If you are ever unsure, **stop and consult a qualified professional.**
